data analytics consultancy proposal for krishna textiles group

so basically we've been talking to krishna textiles about how they're struggling to make sense of all the data they have and why they need proper analytics to compete with the bigger players

the thing is they have 4 manufacturing units across surat and ahmedabad producing around 50000 meters of fabric daily but they literally have no idea which products are profitable and which are bleeding money. their CFO ramesh bhai mentioned in our meeting last friday that they make pricing decisions based on gut feeling and what competitors are charging. they have 6 years of sales data sitting in various excel sheets and tally exports but nobody has ever analyzed it properly. he said they lost ₹2.3 crores last year on a bulk order to reliance trends because they underpriced without understanding their true cost structure

problems they mentioned:
- they have 340+ SKUs but don't know which ones to focus on and which to discontinue
- raw material prices keep fluctuating (cotton especially) but they can't predict or hedge properly
- their 12 salespersons across gujarat and maharashtra are entering orders in whatsapp groups and personal notebooks, zero visibility for management
- inventory management is a nightmare, they have ₹4 crores worth of dead stock in their godown
- they're losing dealers to arvind mills and raymond because those companies give data-driven insights to their channel partners
- GST reconciliation takes 15 days every month because data is scattered everywhere
- the second generation sons harsh and ketan want to modernize but the old team resists any change

what we want to propose

so i was thinking we do this in 4 phases. first we need to consolidate all their data sources which is honestly a mess right now. we'll pull data from their tally ERP, the various excel trackers, their website backend, and even whatsapp order messages. we'll build a proper data warehouse using microsoft azure (they already have office 365 so azure makes sense).

phase 2 is building the core analytics dashboards. we'll create a profitability analyzer that shows margin by product, customer, region and salesperson. a demand forecasting model that predicts orders 3 months ahead based on historical patterns and seasonal trends. and an inventory optimization tool that tells them exactly what to produce and what to clear at discount.

phase 3 is the sales force automation piece. we'll give their salespersons a simple mobile app to log orders, track dealer visits, and see their targets vs achievement. all this data flows directly into the analytics platform so management has real-time visibility.

phase 4 is training and adoption where we actually make sure people use the system. no point building dashboards if ramesh bhai still opens excel every morning.

what they get at the end of all this

unified data platform connecting tally, excel, website and mobile app in one place
profitability dashboard showing exact margin on every meter of fabric sold
demand forecasting with 85% accuracy for 12-week rolling window
automated reorder alerts when raw material stock hits minimum threshold
sales mobile app with offline capability (important for rural dealer visits)
dealer portal where channel partners can see their purchase history and pending orders
GST-ready reports that reduce month-end reconciliation from 15 days to 2 days
WhatsApp integration so orders sent to group automatically create entries
executive mobile dashboard for harsh bhai and ketan bhai to check KPIs anytime
