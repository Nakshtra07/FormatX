cybersecurity compliance services proposal for shriram finance ltd

okay so we had a detailed call with shriram finance about their cybersecurity situation and honestly it's quite concerning given they're an NBFC handling sensitive financial data of lakhs of customers

the thing is RBI has become extremely strict about cybersecurity compliance for NBFCs after that big data breach at another finance company last year. their CISO rajiv menon told me they received a letter from RBI asking them to demonstrate compliance with the new IT governance framework by march 2026 otherwise they face potential license suspension. right now they have basic antivirus and firewalls but nothing close to what the regulator expects. they're processing around 15000 loan applications daily through their app and website and they don't even have proper encryption for data at rest

problems they mentioned:
- they failed their last internal security audit on 23 out of 47 parameters
- their vendor management is non-existent, they share customer data with 8 different fintech partners without any security assessment
- no incident response plan exists, when they had a small phishing attack last quarter nobody knew what to do
- their developers push code directly to production without any security testing whatsoever
- customer KYC documents (aadhaar, pan cards) are stored in plain folders accessible to anyone with network access
- they have 340 employees but zero security awareness training ever conducted
- no SOC (security operations center) capability, they'd never know if someone was inside their network
- ISO 27001 certification expired 2 years ago and was never renewed

what we want to propose

so looking at rbi's master direction on IT framework and their current gaps i'm thinking we do this in 5 phases. first is the comprehensive gap assessment where we audit their entire infrastructure against RBI's cybersecurity framework, CERT-In guidelines, and ISO 27001 standards. we'll give them a heatmap showing exactly where they're red yellow and green.

phase 2 is policy and governance setup. we'll create all the documentation RBI wants - information security policy, access control policy, incident response playbook, vendor risk management framework, data classification policy, business continuity plan. everything their compliance team needs to show the regulator.

phase 3 is technical remediation. we'll implement proper endpoint detection and response (EDR), setup a 24x7 SOC through our partner, deploy data loss prevention (DLP) tools, encrypt all sensitive databases, setup privileged access management for admin accounts, and implement secure SDLC practices with code scanning for their development team.

phase 4 is training and awareness. we'll run mandatory cybersecurity training for all 340 employees with simulated phishing tests. their IT team gets advanced training on incident handling. their developers learn secure coding practices.

phase 5 is audit support where we actually sit with them during the RBI inspection and help answer questions.

security awareness score improvement from current 23% to above 80%
board-level cybersecurity dashboard for quarterly reporting to RBI
secure development pipeline with SAST/DAST integration
