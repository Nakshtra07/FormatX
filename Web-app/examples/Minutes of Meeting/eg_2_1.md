so we started with karthik from client side explaining what they actually want. they need a mobile app for both android and ios that lets their retail customers browse products, place orders, track deliveries and manage their loyalty points. sunita mentioned that the current website gets around 50000 daily active users and they expect the app to handle at least 30000 concurrent users during sale events like diwali and republic day. the app needs to integrate with their existing SAP backend and razorpay for payments.

there was quite a bit of discussion about the scope because initially they wanted chat support but we agreed that would add 3 extra weeks to timeline. sunita said we can add chat in phase 2 instead. rahul raised concerns about the SAP integration because apparently their SAP system is quite old (version from 2018) and the apis are not well documented. karthik promised to get us the documentation by end of this week.

priya showed some initial wireframes she had prepared based on the RFP and everyone liked the bottom navigation approach. however sunita wanted the branding to be more prominent so priya will revise the header section. vikram suggested we use flutter instead of react native because our team has more experience with it and it would be faster to develop. karthik was okay with this as long as performance is not affected.

sneha asked about test devices and karthik confirmed they will provide 5 android phones and 3 iphones of different models for testing. they will courier these to our office by next monday.

what we decided in this meeting

1. agreed to use flutter framework for cross platform development
2. decided to move chat support feature to phase 2 to meet march 31 deadline
3. confirmed the app must support android 9+ and ios 14+ only, no need for older versions
4. approved the bottom navigation UI approach with revised branding header
5. decided to have daily standups at 10am on google meet instead of in-person
6. resolved to conduct weekly demos every friday 4pm with client team
7. finalized that all code will be hosted on client's github enterprise account
