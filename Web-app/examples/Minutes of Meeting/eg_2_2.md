agenda items for today

1. q3 revenue and profit review
2. department wise performance analysis
3. client satisfaction scores
4. employee engagement survey results
5. hiring and attrition update
6. q4 targets and budget allocation
7. any other business

meera started with the sales update. q3 revenue came in at 847 crores which is 12% above target and 18% higher than same quarter last year. she mentioned that the pharma vertical performed exceptionally well contributing 340 crores alone. however the manufacturing sector underperformed, only achieving 78% of target because two large deals got pushed to q4. north region under vijay did really well at 115% of target while west region struggled at 89%.

arun presented delivery metrics. on time delivery improved from 91% to 94%. there were 2 major escalations in q3 compared to 5 in q2 which is a good improvement. resource utilization is at 82% which is healthy. however he flagged that bench strength is low at only 3% and we might struggle if we win the large tata motors deal that's in pipeline. nandini added that customer satisfaction csat scores are at 4.2 out of 5 which is our highest ever.

lakshmi gave hr update. total headcount is now 4,847 employees. we hired 312 people in q3 against target of 350. attrition has reduced from 18% to 14.5% which is much better than industry average of 22%. the recent salary correction in august helped retain some key people who had offers from competitors. employee engagement score from the survey is 72% which is 5 points higher than q2. main concerns raised by employees were around work from home flexibility and career growth paths.

suresh presented the financials. operating margin is 19.2% slightly below the 20% target mainly due to the salary corrections. cash position is strong at 245 crores. collections improved with dso down from 67 days to 58 days. he cautioned that q4 will have higher expenses due to annual bonus payouts and the upcoming company offsite event.

what was decided in the meeting

1. approved - additional hiring budget of 2 crores for q4 to address low bench strength issue
2. approved - special incentive scheme for manufacturing sales team to push q4 deals
3. decided - to conduct skip level meetings in all departments to address career growth concerns raised in survey
4. agreed - hybrid work policy to continue with 3 days office mandatory instead of proposed 4 days
5. resolved - q4 revenue target set at 920 crores which is 8.6% growth over q3
6. approved - client appreciation program budget of 50 lakhs for top 20 accounts
7. deferred - decision on new bangalore office expansion pending real estate cost analysis
