agenda for this meeting

- q3 risk register review and updates
- cyber security incident report
- regulatory compliance status
- dpdp act implementation progress
- internal audit findings
- vendor risk assessment update
- business continuity plan testing results
- upcoming regulatory inspections

shalini started with overall risk dashboard. out of 47 risks in the register, 8 are in red category, 21 in amber and 18 in green. the 3 new red risks added this quarter are - increased cyber threats from ransomware groups targeting our industry, potential gdpr fine from european operations, and supply chain disruption risk due to geopolitical tensions in middle east affecting our raw material suppliers.

sundar gave the cyber security update. we had one significant incident in september where a phishing email compromised 3 employee accounts before it was detected. fortunately no data was exfiltrated but the attackers did access the email inboxes for approximately 6 hours before we locked them out. he has implemented additional email filtering and mandatory phishing simulation training for all employees. also mentioned that our soc team detected and blocked 2,847 attack attempts in q3 which is 34% higher than q2 indicating we are being actively targeted.

arvind presented compliance status. good news is we passed the rbi inspection in october with zero critical findings and only 4 minor observations which are already remediated. however the sebi show cause notice from august is still pending resolution. legal team is preparing our response. on the gst front we have 2 ongoing assessments for fy22 and fy23 with potential exposure of 1.2 crores if department doesn't accept our position.

anita gave dpdp act update. the data protection bill is now law and we have 12 months to become compliant. she has mapped all personal data processing activities across the organization and identified 23 processes that need consent mechanism changes. priority is the customer facing applications which handle data of 4.5 lakh individuals. she flagged that we need budget approval for a consent management platform which will cost approximately 25 lakhs.

nirmala presented internal audit findings. q3 audits covered procurement, payroll and it general controls. procurement had 2 high risk findings - lack of competitive bidding for contracts below 5 lakhs and missing vendor due diligence documentation for 15 vendors. payroll had 1 medium finding about overtime calculation discrepancies. it controls were satisfactory with only minor observations about password policy not being enforced for legacy applications.

venkatesh shared bcp drill results. the annual business continuity test was conducted in october simulating a scenario where mumbai office becomes inaccessible. 87% of critical functions were able to continue within the 4 hour rto target. main gaps were in the call center operations where only 65% of agents could work from home due to connectivity issues and in finance where one critical application doesn't have remote access capability.

what the committee decided

1. approved - emergency budget of 25 lakhs for dpdp act consent management platform implementation
2. resolved - mandatory phishing awareness training for all employees to be completed by dec 31
3. decided - procurement policy to be revised requiring competitive bidding for all purchases above 2 lakhs instead of current 5 lakhs
4. agreed - all vendors without proper due diligence documentation to be suspended from new orders until documentation is completed
5. approved - additional headcount of 2 people for soc team given increased threat landscape
6. decided - it team to enable remote access for critical finance application within 60 days
7. agreed - call center work from home connectivity issue to be escalated to ceo for budget approval
